 What is Crypto? 

Crypto is short for Cryptography which is the study or process of creating secret code. Crypto is also short for Cryptocurrency which is a digital currency that utilizes cryptography and a decentralized monetary system.

What is a crib? A baby’s cradle? A place to crash for the night?

As described by Wikipedia, a crib is “a plain language (or code) passage of any length, usually obtained by solving one or more cipher or code messages, and occurring or believed likely to occur in a different cipher or code message, which it may provide a means of solving.”

Cribbing means to figure out the crib after it has been encoded as ciphertext (secret code). But you have to know some partial information about the crib, such as it’s first or last character or how many characters it has in total. A Crypto Crib, therefore, is a place for your Bitcoin to crash, right?

Not really. It just means we are going to discuss the secrets of cryptocurrencies - right here in The Crypto Crib.

Crypto Terms

Cryptocurrency – digital currency that is represented by a token and held for investment or traded on a blockchain network.

Altcoin – any cryptocurrency that is not Bitcoin. There are over 9,000 altcoins as of 2021.

Bitcoin (symbol: BTC) – launched in 2009 by Satoshi Nakamoto as a platform for peer-to-peer electronic cash transactions. Bitcoin solved the problem of double spending through its proof-of-work consensus mechanism.

Blockchain Technology – a decentralized distributed ledger system that powers cryptocurrency transactions and smart contracts. Transaction records are simultaneously stored on multiple computers called nodes that are located throughout the world. Every node must validate and agree on every transaction which results in a secure blockchain network. Transactions on a blockchain network are immutable which means they are not able to be changed.

Smart contract – a self-executing contract between two or more parties with terms written into the code, including event triggers to automatically execute those terms.

Digital Wallet– aka crypto wallet, hot wallet, soft wallet, hard wallet, cold wallet – examples of hot/cold

Double spending – In digital cash, the potential of someone spending the same token twice.

Ethereum (symbol: ETH) – Proof-of-Work cryptocurrency that is also a platform for other decentralized applications to build on

DeFi (or “decentralized finance”) is an umbrella term for financial services on public blockchains, primarily Ethereum. With DeFi, you can do most of the things that banks support — earn interest, borrow, lend, buy insurance, trade derivatives, trade assets, and more — but it’s faster and doesn’t require paperwork or a third party. - Coinbase, July 2021. Satoshi Nakamoto – creator of Bitcoin, author of the Bitcoin white paper; famously unknown – could be a man, a woman, or a group of people

Bitcoin

Bitcoin is the world’s original cryptocurrency. Satoshi Nakamoto published the Bitcoin white paper in 2008, and the coin was launched in 2009. The first Bitcoin transaction took place in 2010 when a Florida man, Laszlo Hanyez, paid 10,000 Bitcoins to a man in Great Britain who agreed to buy two large Papa John’s pizza for the Mr. Hanyez. The transaction was worth $41 at the time but would be worth over $300 million in 2021. Bitcoin Pizza Day on May 22 was named in honor of this first transaction and celebrated every year.
